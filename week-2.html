Personalized Bowl for Winston

Winston, the cheeky neighborhood cat of East Capitol Hill, was the inspiration for my next piece – a personalized bowl. This project was an exercise in creating an object that's not only functional but also carries a personal touch.

Designing Winston’s bowl was a lesson in the nuances of user-centric design!

The extrude function was my main tool for shaping the initial form of the bowl. Starting with a simple sketch, I carefully extruded the base shape, paying close attention to the bowl’s depth and slope. The challenge was to create a bowl with sides that would allow easy access to food without strain on Winston’s posture. Through a series of extrusions, I shaped a bowl that gradually contoured inward, an inviting form for a sweet cat to dip its head into!

With the basic shape in place, my attention shifted to refining the bowl’s rim. My primary concern was Winston’s ease of use, particularly the comfort of his chin while eating. With this in mind, I made bevels along the edges. This process involved a delicate balance—each bevel had to be smooth enough to provide comfort, yet precise to maintain the bowl’s form. I iterated several profiles, each time assessing the bevel's angle and the resulting contour. Achieving the right bevel meant observing how different chamfer sizes interacted with the bowl’s overall shape and Winston’s potential movements.

The addition of fillets and chamfers further enhanced the bowl's comfort and safety features, ensuring that it's as pleasing to use as it is to the eye. The embossed name 'WINSTON' wraps around the bowl, giving it a bespoke feel that I hope is appreciated by its four-legged recipient!
