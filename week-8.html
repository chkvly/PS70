CNC and Vacuum Forming Fabrication of a Cat Tray

Design and Planning

  • Design Inspiration and Digital Tracing: A photo of Winston, the cat, was used to digitally trace and create a file compatible with CNC machining, ensuring the final tray reflected Winstonâ€™s silhouette.

CNC Machining

  • Plywood was accurately cut using a CNC machine. Initial inaccuracies required a second machining pass to achieve perfect mold edges for the vacuum forming process.

Vacuum Forming

  • A plastic sheet was heated and then vacuum-formed over the CNC-cut plywood to create a detailed and durable mold. This mold captured all the nuances of the design, allowing for high-quality plaster casting.

Casting

  • The detailed plastic mold was prepared and plaster mixed with purple dye was poured in, ensuring consistency in the marbled effect by carefully stirring to prevent air bubbles.

Demolding and Finishing

  • Once the plaster set, the piece was demolded. Any imperfections were sanded, and edges smoothed.
  • A coat of Rust-Oleum Triple Thick Glaze was applied for a glossy finish and added protection.
